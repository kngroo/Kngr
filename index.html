About

I like trying out new tech. I love taking apart and putting back together said tech to learn then develop my own ideas. My classmate from college gave me an old Bianchi road bike from the 70's that was completely rusted over from being left out in the cold Toronto winter. When the weather got warmer, I brought the bike to an on-campus DIY community bike repair space, and completely disassembled it, down to the last ball bearing. After hours of cleaning, lubricating, and replacing broken parts, the reassembled bike, although not without patina, meticulously tuned to perfection by me, rode better than majority of the mass produced bikes in stores. From that moment on, I became the guy to see for bikes amongst my group of friends, and their friends. I estimate that I have restored close to twenty bikes since then. I carry the same passion and curiosity for programming, and employ the same methodology to dissect and learn new technologies.
